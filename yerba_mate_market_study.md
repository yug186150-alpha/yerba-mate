## 3. Types of Yerba Mate

### Yerba Mate Tereré
Cold-brewed yerba mate, extremely popular in **Paraguay** and parts of Brazil/Argentina. Served with ice water or fruit juice, often enhanced with fresh herbs, mint, or citrus. Ideal for hot climates — highly relevant for the Indian market. Best served with **Paraguayan-style** yerba (Pajarito, Selecta, Campesino).

### Yerba Mate Chimarrão
A **Brazilian specialty** — finely pulverized, bright green, unaged leaves with minimal or no stems. Has a mild, grassy, creamy flavour. Requires specific technique due to its powdery consistency. Top brands: **Barão de Cotegipe, Yacuy, Ximango**.

### French Press Yerba Mate
A convenient Western-style alternative to the traditional gourd-and-bombilla method. Uses ~1 tablespoon per cup, steeped in hot water (65°C–82°C) for 4–8 minutes. Ideal for beginners. Works best with **Argentine-style** yerba (medium cut with stems). Note: very fine cuts (chimarrão, Uruguayan) may clog the press.

### 🇦🇷 Argentine Brands

#### Cruz de Malta
- One of Argentina's most popular and widely exported yerba mate brands. Balanced mix of leaves, stems, and minimal dust. Smooth, slightly sweet/buttery flavour. **Excellent beginner brand.** Available in India via Desertcart and Ubuy (expect premium pricing due to import costs).

#### Guayakí Yerba Mate
- **HQ:** California, USA (founded 1996) | **Structure:** Certified B Corporation, Social Purpose Corporation
- **Estimated Revenue: $100M+** (privately held, exact figures not disclosed)
- North America's leading yerba mate brand. Offers RTD cans/bottles, loose-leaf, and tea bags. Operates on a **"Market Driven Regeneration"** model — sourcing from regenerative farming communities in Argentina, Paraguay, and Brazil. Major distribution across Whole Foods, convenience stores, and online.
- **CEO:** Ben Mand (as of 2024)

#### Rosamonte
- Traditional Argentine brand, known for strong, bold, smoky, and woody character. The **"Suave"** variant is mild and smooth — good for beginners. The **"Especial"** (aged 24 months) is complex — for experts.

#### Taragüi
- Argentina's best-selling yerba mate brand. Classic, reliable, accessible flavour. Good for beginners.

#### CBSé
- Argentina's leading **flavoured yerba mate** brand. Extensive range: Naranja (orange), Pomelo (grapefruit), Limón, Frutos del Bosque (berry), Frutos Tropicales, Hierbas Serranas, Energía (guaraná), Miel (honey), and Endulife (stevia-sweetened).

#### Playadito
- Sweet, herbal, and approachable. Frequently recommended as a **top beginner** brand.

#### Kraus
- Organic, air-dried, zero-smoke yerba mate. Clean, mellow profile — great for beginners seeking a pure taste.

---

### 🇺🇾 Uruguayan Brands

#### Canarias
- **The benchmark** for Uruguayan-style mate. Intense, bold, long-lasting, creamy texture. Very fine, powdery consistency. Dominant in Uruguay's domestic market and international exports. **Expert-level** brand — can be overwhelming for beginners.

#### Canarias Serena (Special Flavour)
- A **compuesta (blended)** variant of Canarias. Infused with **linden (tilo), passionflower (mburucuyá), mint, and lemon balm (melisa)**. Smoother, calmer, and milder than standard Canarias. Ideal for afternoon sessions or those who find classic Canarias too intense. Stemless, finely cut.

#### Sara
- Established 1918, managed by Carrau y Cia. A premium P.U.1 grade product. Balanced bitterness with a sweet finish — slightly softer than Canarias.

#### Del Cebador
- Owned by Molino Puritas (Rapenor S.A.). Rich, malty, sometimes chocolatey notes. Full-bodied, consistent — popular daily drinker for experienced users.

---

### 🇵🇾 Paraguayan Brands

#### Pajarito
- Founded 1956 in Bella Vista, Itapúa. Considered a **national symbol of Paraguay**. Traditional barbacuá (smoke-dried) processing. Full control from plantation to packaging. Diverse range includes organic, blended, instant, and tea bag formats. **First Paraguayan company to sell yerba in tea bags.** Excellent for tereré.

#### Selecta (Eno Bronstrup S.A.)
- Established 1950. Known for outstanding **price-to-quality ratio** and extensive product range. Numerous herbal and fruit-flavoured blends specifically designed for tereré. Widely available internationally.

#### Campesino
- Founded 1980s. Bold, intense flavour profile. Famous for creative herb/fruit blends (mint, boldo, citrus). Balances traditional Paraguayan methods with modern consumer preferences. Great for both hot mate and tereré.

### Key Requirements for Commercial Partnerships

- Certifications: FSSAI (India), USDA/EU Organic, HACCP, IFS, BRCGS
- Phytosanitary certificates for Indian customs
- Indian food labeling compliance

## 9. Buy Bulk from Plantations vs Buy from Established Brands

### Option A: Buy Bulk Directly from South American Plantations

This means sourcing raw/semi-processed yerba mate directly from plantation owners, cooperatives, or export houses in Argentina, Paraguay, or Brazil — then packaging and branding it yourself in India.

#### How to Find Bulk Plantation Suppliers

| Channel | Details |
|---|---|
| **Nutrada** (nutrada.com) | Certified bulk yerba mate exporter. Offers cut & sifted, powder, and whole leaf. Provides Certificates of Analysis (COA) per batch. |
| **Tridge** (tridge.com) | Global agricultural trade platform. Filter by origin (Argentina, Paraguay, Brazil), certifications, and MOQ. |
| **go4WorldBusiness** | B2B marketplace connecting buyers with South American yerba mate exporters and cooperatives. |
| **Directoro** (directoro.com) | International food ingredient sourcing platform with verified suppliers. |
| **Alibaba** | Bulk yerba mate listings from Argentine/Brazilian producers. Verify supplier credentials carefully. |
| **Las Marías (Taragüi)** | Argentina's largest producer. Has a dedicated export division for B2B bulk orders. Contact directly. |
| **Hreñuk S.A. (Rosamonte)** | Major Argentine manufacturer with export capability. |
| **Eno Bronstrup S.A. (Selecta)** | Paraguayan manufacturer with own plantations in Bella Vista and San Rafael. |
| **Trade Shows** | Attend **Anuga** (Germany), **SIAL** (Paris), or **Expo Comidas Latinas** to meet plantation owners and exporters face-to-face. |

#### Bulk Pricing & MOQs

| Detail | Estimate |
|---|---|
| **Bulk Price (FOB Argentina)** | Negotiated per contract — varies by grade, organic status, and cut. Significantly lower than retail (expect bulk rates well below ₹300–400/kg before shipping). |
| **MOQ (Small Wholesale)** | 25–30 kg from some traders/intermediaries |
| **MOQ (Direct Plantation)** | 1–20 metric tonnes (full container loads for best pricing) |
| **Typical Packaging** | 25kg multi-layered kraft paper bags with PE liners |
| **Moisture Requirement** | <8% for export compliance |
| **Certifications Available** | Organic (USDA NOP, EU), Fair Trade, HACCP, FSSC 22000, BRCGS, IFS |

#### What You Need to Specify When Ordering
- **Form:** Cut & sifted (C/S), powder, or whole leaf
- **Grade:** Con palo (with stems) or sin palo (without stems)
- **Processing:** Smoke-dried (barbacuá) vs. air-dried vs. traditional
- **Aging:** 12–18 months for premium quality
- **Incoterms:** Request both FOB and CIF quotes to compare landed costs
- **Certifications:** FSSAI-compliant, organic, fair-trade as needed

#### Documents Your Supplier Must Provide
- Phytosanitary certificate (mandatory for Indian customs)
- Certificate of Analysis (caffeine assay, moisture, microbiological report)
- Commercial invoice & packing list
- Certificate of Origin
- Bill of Lading

### Option B: Buy from Established Brands (Resell)

This means purchasing finished, branded yerba mate products (Cruz de Malta, Taragüi, Pajarito, etc.) from distributors or international e-commerce, and reselling them in India under those brand names.

| Factor | Details |
|---|---|
| **How** | Purchase via international distributors, Desertcart wholesale, Ubuy, or direct from brand export departments |
| **Branding** | You sell under **their brand name** — no own-brand identity |
| **Margins** | Lower margins due to retail markup already baked in |
| **MOQ** | Low — can start with small quantities (cases/cartons) |
| **Ease** | Very easy to start, minimal infrastructure needed |
| **Limitations** | No control over product, pricing, or packaging. Brand loyalty goes to them, not you. |

### ⭐ Recommended Strategy: Buy Bulk + Own Brand + Value-Add

> [!TIP]
> **Best approach for maximum profit and brand building:**
> 1. **Source bulk** yerba mate directly from Argentine/Paraguayan plantations or export houses (Las Marías, Hreñuk, EBSA, or via Nutrada/Tridge)
> 2. **Import raw material** into India (lower duty than finished goods)
> 3. **Blend locally** with Indian flavours — cardamom, ginger, tulsi, masala, mint — to create unique India-specific products
> 4. **Package under your own brand** with premium design
> 5. **Sell via** Amazon.in, Flipkart, gourmet stores, wellness cafes, D2C website
>
> This gives you **the best margins, full brand control, and a unique product** that no imported brand can offer.

### Hybrid Approach (Recommended for Launch)

| Phase | Strategy |
|---|---|
| **Phase 1 (Month 1–3)** | Start by reselling established brands (Cruz de Malta, Playadito) to test market demand with low investment |
| **Phase 2 (Month 3–6)** | Once demand is validated, negotiate bulk purchase from a plantation/exporter, order samples, verify quality |
| **Phase 3 (Month 6–12)** | Launch your own branded yerba mate — plain + India-flavoured blends. Continue stocking popular imported brands alongside |
| **Phase 4 (Year 2+)** | Scale own brand, expand to RTD products, wholesale to cafes and wellness stores |
